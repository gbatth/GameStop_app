Betting on "Wall Street Bets"

A "Sentiment Analysis" on the subreddit "Wall Street Bets?" with a focus on the recent "GameStop" saga.

  • Get Started

What is a Sentiment Analysis?

Sentiment analysis is a natural language processing (NLP) technique used to determine positive, negative or neutral trends on any given topic. In other words, it is the process of "opinion mining" performed generally on textual data to monitor human "sentiments."

Hypothesis

Sentiment Analysis can be performed on any topic/product to gain customer insight. We chose the WallStreetBets and GamesStop saga due to the attention it has been getting recently. We wanted to test the hypothesis whether analysis of the January 2021 "top" comments on WallStreetBets would lead us to the same conclusion as the recent buyers of GameStop stock.

Methodology

We performed two independent analyses to test our hypothesis. First, we used PSAW Scraper to scrape API data from r/WallStreetBets? and summarized the usage of the following keywords/phrases(GME, GameStop, "hold the line", "to the moon") within it. We then compared it to the stock price development of GameStop (using NasDaq data) in the same time period.Second, we utilized python's Natural Language ToolKit's (NLTK) Vader Sentiment Analyzer to calculate the stock sentiments within r/WallStreetBets? for the month of January. We limited our data to comments that received 20 or more "Upvotes".

Results

The results of the Sentiment Analysis were surprisingly accurate in the fact that it was in the first place. However, what was unique was that even though it lead on both the bulls and the bears most of the refernces were neutral. Therefore, an avid investor would probably have not invested into GameStop.
